# Where to Live in Cheshire?

Cheshire is not one market — it is three, stacked by budget, commute tolerance, and what you are willing to trade off. The county stretches from the outer edge of Greater Manchester's commuter belt down to the Welsh border, and the difference in price between the top and bottom of that range is roughly £525,000. Getting the right answer to where you should live depends on which of those three zones fits your situation.

<!-- INTRO -->

The top tier is the Golden Triangle: Wilmslow, Alderley Edge, and Prestbury. These are the towns that draw the highest earners out of Manchester and the Londoners who arrive with significant equity. Alderley Edge averages above £700,000 and Prestbury above £650,000. Schools are consistently rated Outstanding, trains to Manchester Piccadilly run every 15 to 20 minutes, and the fastest journey from Wilmslow takes 17 minutes. *(Source: National Rail, 2025)* If you have a budget above £600,000 and a Manchester commute to manage, this is the rational choice — not a lifestyle indulgence.

The middle band covers Knutsford, Macclesfield, Chester, and Nantwich. These towns offer much of the same school quality and access at 30 to 50 percent lower prices. Macclesfield is 25 minutes from Manchester Piccadilly and averages £280,000. Chester is the county's only true city, with its own employment base, a direct train to London Euston, and an average price of £250,000 — though the Manchester commute at roughly 50 minutes is the compromise. This band is where most relocators from London end up, particularly those bringing £400,000 to £650,000 from a sale in zone 2 or 3.

The accessible tier — Northwich at £220,000 and Crewe at £175,000 — serves buyers who are either working locally, working from home most of the week, or prioritising mortgage size over commute time. Crewe has direct trains to Manchester and London but the town itself is industrial in character and the surrounding housing stock is a different proposition from the villages further north. Northwich sits between the two larger zones and has improved meaningfully over the past decade, though it lacks the independent retail scene of Knutsford or the school density of the Golden Triangle.

The typical relocator arriving from London is 35 to 45 years old, selling a flat or smaller house in zones 2 to 4, and arriving with equity of between £400,000 and £900,000. That money buys a detached house with a garden and a school catchment that would cost three to four times as much in Surrey or Hertfordshire — London's average sits at £551,294 against Cheshire East's £306,000. *(Source: ONS / Land Registry UKHPI, December 2025)* The county rewards that move well — but only if you land in the right area for your particular situation.

## Questions from People Making the Move

**RL — Clapham, London**

*"We've got £750k from our London flat — where in Cheshire gives us the best schools AND a reasonable commute?"*

At £750,000 you are firmly inside Golden Triangle territory. Wilmslow gives you the fastest commute — 17 minutes to Manchester Piccadilly *(Source: National Rail, 2025)* — and a concentration of Outstanding-rated primary and secondary schools within a compact catchment. Alderley Edge and Prestbury are within the same budget at the lower end of their ranges and offer a more village-scale environment if that matters to you. The key decision is whether you want to be on a train in under 20 minutes or whether a slightly longer drive to Knutsford (35 minutes by train) would give you more house for the same money.

**JM — Didsbury, Manchester**

*"We're priced out of Altrincham but want the same quality of life. What's the Cheshire equivalent?"*

Knutsford is the closest match in character — a compact, walkable town centre, good independent food and retail, strong schools, and a broadly professional demographic. The average price sits around £430,000, which is below Altrincham's comparable stock. Poynton is worth looking at too: it sits just inside Cheshire East, averages £444,312 with a 20-minute direct train to Manchester Piccadilly, and it tends to be overlooked by people searching further into the county. *(Source: Rightmove, 2025; Northern Trains, current timetable)*

## Questions People Ask Before Moving to Cheshire

**Is Cheshire expensive to live in?**

It depends which part. Cheshire East averages £306,000 across all property types *(Source: ONS, November 2025)*, but that figure masks a wide range — from Crewe at around £175,000 to Alderley Edge above £700,000. Day-to-day costs in the county are broadly in line with the national average outside of the premium towns, where restaurants and independent schools push spending up significantly.

**Is Cheshire a good place to raise a family?**

The county has a high density of Outstanding and Good Ofsted schools, particularly in Cheshire East, and most of the main towns have accessible parks, sports clubs, and youth facilities. The constraint is that outside the larger towns, getting children to activities depends almost entirely on car ownership — public transport is limited outside the main rail corridors. Across both authorities, 76 schools hold Outstanding Ofsted ratings — roughly 22%, compared with 16% nationally. *(Source: SNobe / Ofsted, 2024-25; GOV.UK, August 2025)*

**What is the Golden Triangle in Cheshire?**

The Golden Triangle refers to the roughly triangular area bounded by Wilmslow, Alderley Edge, and Prestbury — three towns in the northeast of the county with the highest concentration of expensive property, high-earning residents, and premium schools. Average prices across the three exceed £490,000 in Wilmslow and go above £700,000 in Alderley Edge. The label is used loosely in estate agent copy, but the price data bears it out.

**How long is the commute from Cheshire to Manchester?**

It varies significantly by town. The fastest is Wilmslow at 17 minutes to Manchester Piccadilly *(Source: National Rail, 2025)*. Prestbury and Alderley Edge are 20 and 22 minutes respectively. Macclesfield is 25 minutes. Chester is around 50 minutes. Towns like Tarporley and Nantwich have no direct service and require a car to a connecting station, putting the total journey over an hour.

**Is it worth moving from London to Cheshire?**

For most households selling in inner or mid London, the equity released by the move is substantial — a flat that sold for £600,000 in zone 2 buys a four-bedroom detached house in Wilmslow with money left over. The practical trade-offs are real: London's cultural and professional density does not exist in Cheshire, and if your work requires regular in-person presence in central London, the journey is significant. For Manchester commuters, the calculation is much cleaner.

**What is the cost of living in Cheshire compared to London?**

Housing is the main difference — Cheshire East averages £306,000 against London's £551,294 *(Source: ONS / Land Registry UKHPI, December 2025)*. Groceries, utilities, and everyday services are broadly comparable or marginally cheaper. Private school fees, restaurant prices, and leisure costs in the premium towns are closer to London levels. Council tax Band D runs £2,332.61 in Cheshire East (slightly above the £2,280 England average) and £1,978.09 in Cheshire West and Chester (well below). *(Source: GOV.UK, 2025-26)*

## Good to Know

Cheshire has real downsides that do not appear in area guides written by people trying to sell you something. Car dependency is significant: outside Wilmslow, Knutsford, Macclesfield, and Chester, you will drive everywhere — to the supermarket, to the school run, to the station. Households with one car or no car will find large parts of the county impractical. The evening economy is thin outside Chester; most towns close by 9pm and the restaurant scene, while improving, is not comparable to a Manchester suburb. There is also a well-documented postcode hierarchy between areas — the gap in perceived status between Alderley Edge and Wilmslow, or between Prestbury and Poynton, is felt more keenly locally than it looks on a map, and it feeds into school gate culture and property pricing in ways that are worth understanding before you commit to a street.
